Norway's Nature
Scenery

Norway is home to some of the most dramatic and unspoiled landscapes on Earth. From thundering waterfalls cascading down sheer cliff faces to the hushed, snow-dusted plateaus of the far north, the country's terrain was shaped by millions of years of glacial carving and the relentless pull of the sea.

Interactive 3D model Geirangerfjord · 1:125,000 scale

The Geirangerfjord, shown above, is one of Norway's most celebrated natural wonders and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Stretching 15 kilometres inland, its sheer cliffs rise over 1,400 metres above the water. Waterfalls such as the Seven Sisters and the Suitor cascade down its walls year-round, fed by snowmelt from the peaks above.

Beyond the fjords, Norway's landscapes span the vast Hardangervidda plateau — Europe's largest mountain plain — all the way to the Arctic archipelago of Svalbard, where polar bears roam freely and the midnight sun never sets for months at a time. These wild places are protected as part of Norway's deep national commitment to preserving its natural heritage for generations to come.
