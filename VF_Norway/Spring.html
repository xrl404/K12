Norway's Freshwater
Springs

Norway is renowned for its abundant and pristine freshwater springs — an integral part of the country's natural landscape. These springs often emerge in mountainous and forested areas, fed by melting snow, glaciers, and underground aquifers, making them some of the cleanest sources of drinking water in Europe.

Many Norwegian springs have very low levels of pollutants and minerals, resulting in crisp, clear water sometimes bottled commercially. Historically significant for drinking, agriculture, and traditional health practices, they are now protected under strict environmental regulations — ensuring they remain unspoiled habitats and beloved destinations for hikers and nature explorers alike.
